Slide 2 — Why Companies Should Care

Three Risks That Drive
Supplier Social Audits

Why companies take an active interest in the social performance of their supply chains.

01

Protect Their Reputation

A brand is only as strong as its weakest link. If a supplier uses child labor or has unsafe factories, the public blames the buying company — not the supplier.

The Risk Negative media and social media backlash can destroy customer trust and loyalty overnight.
02

Ensure Stable Supply

Suppliers that treat workers well have happier, healthier, and more stable workforces — meaning reliable production.

The Risk High turnover, strikes, or factory shutdowns for safety violations lead to delays, shortages, and lost sales.
03

Comply with Laws

Laws like the German Supply Chain Act and Modern Slavery Acts hold companies legally responsible for human rights violations in their supply chain.

The Risk Ignoring supplier misconduct can lead to massive government fines, court cases, and legal expenses.
Slide 3 — What Can Be Done

Two Actions Companies
Can Take

Practical steps to manage and improve supplier social performance.

Action 01

Conduct Social Audits & Certifications

Implement regular, independent third-party audits of suppliers using recognized standards like SA8000, BSCI, or Sedex/SMETA.

  • 1 Map the full supply chain to identify high-risk tiers and regions
  • 2 Commission unannounced audits by independent bodies — not self-assessments
  • 3 Require corrective action plans with verified deadlines for non-conformities
  • 4 Publish audit results for transparency and stakeholder trust
Action 02

Build Long-Term Supplier Partnerships

Move beyond policing to partnerships: invest in supplier development programs that raise social standards from within.

  • 1 Co-fund training on worker rights, safety standards, and fair wages
  • 2 Establish worker grievance hotlines and anonymous reporting channels
  • 3 Commit to fair purchasing practices — avoid squeezing margins that force corners to be cut
  • 4 Reward top-performing suppliers with longer contracts and preferred status
Non-Conformity Detected → Risk Assessed → Audit & Certify → Partner & Develop → Sustainable Supply Chain
